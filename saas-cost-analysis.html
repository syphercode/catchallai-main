Pricing as ofQ2 2026

If a single integrated platform replaced these fourteen apps, the savings line is the entire stack — not a row of it. This ledger shows the realistic annual cost a 100-person company pays for the leading commercial product in each category.

The realistic-team-size method. Most of these tools don't have 100 seats — a 100-person company has 5 SEO seats, 4 social media seats, and 100 chat seats. Each row uses a defensible team size for a 100-person mid-market company.

Mid-market enterprise tier. Pricing reflects the tier a 100-person company would actually buy (Salesforce Enterprise, not Starter; Slack Business+, not Pro). All numbers are list-price annual; negotiated discounts of 10–30% are common but excluded here so totals don't undercount.

License-only. Implementation, integration, training, admin headcount, and renewal increases are excluded. Real first-year totals are typically 1.5–2× higher for systems like Workday, NetSuite, and Salesforce.

Honest Caveats

This number is right for the wrong reasons — and wrong for the right ones.

Most companies don't actually buy all of these. Many run on Salesforce + HubSpot + Slack + Workday + Asana and call it a day. Skipping the dedicated SEO, CLM, GRC, and recognition tools cuts the number by 25–35%.

Implementation isn't priced here. Workday, NetSuite, and Salesforce implementations run 100–200% of annual license in year one. Real first-year for this stack is closer to $1.0–1.2M, not $638K.

Negotiation is real. List price is the start. A 100-person company committing to multi-year contracts typically lands 15–25% below list. Apply that and the realistic license-only run-rate is ~$510K/year.

Bundled platforms blur categories. Workday HCM bundles Greenhouse-equivalent recruiting; Salesforce bundles Slack; Microsoft 365 bundles Teams. Some categories are already consolidated upstream.

The "free" tier is a trap. GA4 is free; the cost is the engineer hours fixing tracking. NetSuite includes "free" reporting; the cost is the consultant who builds the reports. Hidden labor cost on top of license is real.

Aerospace/defense premium. ITAR/CMMC/FedRAMP-compliant tiers are typically 20–40% more expensive, and not every vendor offers them. The defense premium adds another $100–150K to a stack like this.

Renewal increases compound. SaaS renewals routinely come in at 7–15% YoY without a price-cap clause. Five years of compounding turns a $638K stack into ~$900K by year five even with stable headcount.

"Replace" rarely means 1:1 replacement. A unified platform doesn't have to match Workday's depth in payroll or NetSuite's depth in revenue recognition to be useful. The honest comparison is "replaces 80% of the value at 30% of the cost," not feature parity.
